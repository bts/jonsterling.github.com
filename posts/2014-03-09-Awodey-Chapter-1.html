Awodey Exercises, Chapter 1.

Some exercises from Awodey’s Category Theory.

  1. The objects of \(\mathbf{Rel}\) are sets, and an arrow \(A\to b\) is a relation from \(A\) to \(B\), that is, a subset \(R\subseteq A\times B\). The equality relation \(\{\langle a,a\rangle\in A\times A \mid a\in A\}\) is the identity arrow on a set \(A\). Composition on \(\mathbf{Rel}\) is to be given by \[ S\circ R \mapsto \{\langle a,c\rangle\in A\times C\mid \exists b. \langle a,b\rangle\in R\land \langle b,c\rangle\in S\} \]
    1. \(\mathbf{Rel}\) is a category.

      1. For all arrows \(S: A\to B\), \(1_B\circ S = S\). \[\begin{align} 1_B\circ S &= \{\langle b,b\rangle\in B\times B\mid b\in B\} \circ S \\ &= \{\langle a,b\rangle\in A\times B\mid \exists b'. \langle a,b'\rangle\in S\land \langle b',b\rangle\in 1_B\}\\ &= \{\langle a,b\rangle\in A\times B\mid \langle a,b\rangle\in S\} \tag{by the definition of $1_B$}\\ &= S \tag{by the axiom of extensionality} \end{align}\]

      2. For all arrows \(S: A\to B\), \(S\circ1_A = S\). \[\begin{align} S\circ1_A &= S\circ \{\langle a,a\rangle\in A\times A\mid a\in A\}\\ &= \{\langle a,b\rangle\in A\times B\mid \exists a'. \langle a',a\rangle\in 1_A\land\langle a',b\rangle\in S\}\\ &= \{\langle a,b\rangle\in A\times B\mid \langle a,b\rangle\in S\}\tag{by the definition of $1_A$}\\ &= S \tag{by the axiom of extensionality} \end{align}\]

      3. For all arrows \(R: A\to B\), \(S: B\to C\), \(T: C\to D\), we have \(T\circ (S\circ R) = (T\circ S) \circ R\).
